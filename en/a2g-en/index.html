The Client

A2G is a Peruvian consulting firm that helps companies understand, measure, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. One of their company pillars is environmental communication. I interned in-house at A2G, where I helped create various communication pieces.

Projects:

METRICO2

Metrico2 is the carbon footprint measurement software created by A2G. For launch, we developed a video explainer highlighting the main features and benefits of it.

The project

The main objective of the video was to advertise the software to the target audience: current and potential clients of A2G, as well as stakeholders interested in the study of carbon footprints.

There were four specific goals for this project:

  • Emphasize the importance of measuring the carbon footprint for a business.
  • Highlight A2G's expertise in measuring and managing carbon footprints.
  • Announce the launch of METRICO2
  • Communicate the benefits of using METRICO2.

My role

My duties consisted first of co-writing the script. Then I created all the visual metaphors needed to illustrate some more abstract concepts.

I participated in the development of the assets for the video. For the illustrations, I followed the style laid out by my supervisor: gradients and stylized human figures, inspired by the trends in web illustration and software at that moment (2019). We also relied heavily on METRICO2's visual style and UI design.

Finally, I was also responsible for the animation and editing of the video.

The biggest challenge in this project was communicating technical information in a way that would sell the product. The video script had to go through several iterations before being approved. Taking into account the final audience was essential to achieve this balance.

You can watch the video on A2G's website.

Comparative emissions

Inspired by this video made by Carbon Visuals and the Environmental Defense Fund, A2G wanted to make a similar video with the same premise (spheres of 1 ton of CO2e compared to spaces in a city) but using landmarks of Lima and Peru to give a better point of comparison to a Peruvian audience.

Objectives

The objective was to illustrate the magnitude of CO2 emissions in Peru. Its goal was to create awareness about the issue and encourage the audience to reflect on it.

My role

I helped write the script and translate data into landmarks easily recognizable by the Peruvian public. I was also entirely in charge of the 3D animation of the video, using particles for the spheres and free-to-use models from Google maps.

All the elements and buildings in the video are to scale. I also made sure to include only the most accurate models I could find.

The biggest challenge was maintaining the accuracy and scale of the representations. I had to work hand in hand with my colleagues to make the equivalences as close to reality as possible.

The video is still on the A2G website.
