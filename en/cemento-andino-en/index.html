The client

Cemento Andino is part of the cementos UNACEM group. The brand has various cement products tailored to construction workers and their craft.

The project

In the context of the bicentenary of Peruvian independence, Cemento Andino decided to produce an animated video commemorating the event and reinforcing the brand as a Peruvian product. Besides highlighting its core values, the main objective was to bring the brand closer to its target audience, making a parallel between Peruvian culture, its legacy and the legacy that construction workers build with their labour.

Considering all this, I mapped out the fundamental objectives for the project:

  • Communicate and reinforce the identity of the brand as proudly Peruvian.
  • Celebrate the bicentenary relating it to the concept of the brand's legacy.
  • Highlight all the Peruvian aspects of the brand, representing its multiculturalism.

The process

My main tasks consisted of developing a visual style for the video and designing and illustrating all the screens. From the beginning, the brief was clear: the final product needed to exude Peruvian culture.

With that in mind, I collected references and photographs of multiple regions of the country and its people. Putting it all together, I created a mood board that served as a guide for the graphics.

In terms of colour, I built the colour palette based on different pre-Hispanic ceramics and art. To bring the colours closer to the brand, I included shades and tints of the brand colours, black, light blue, red and green, as accents. I used the saturated brand colours to make the characters pop against the terracotta background.

As with colour, The final illustration style gathered inspiration from textile and ceramic patterns from two specific pre-Columbian cultures: Chancay and Wari. Mixing their heavy use of triangles, diagonal lines, rhombus, and stair-like motifs with the pre-existing brand's designs was crucial to achieving the final result seen in the video.

The character illustration was stylized and inspired by cuchimilcos and similar pre-hispanic ceramic figures. One of the biggest challenges was maintaining the overall stylized and simplified look while portraying some details as accurate to real life as possible. Since the video's target audience were construction workers, the accurate portrayal of construction processes was crucial, especially in security matters. For example, I needed to include protective gear or harnesses in all the illustrations where the main character was working on a building.

The final product went live on Facebook at the end of July 2021. The final video can be found here.
