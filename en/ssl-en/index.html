Salud sin Limites is a non-profit focused on promoting the development of public policies related to health, gender equality and the fight against poverty. Together with the Spanish non-profit Medicusmundi and with the support of the Government of Navarra and the Felipe Rinaldi Foundation, Salud sin Limites carried out the program "A comprehensive and inclusive health for Huancavelica" to implement the Model of Comprehensive Health Care Based on Family and Community (MAIS BFC).

The project

Salud sin Límites wanted to make an explanatory video showing the differences in opportunities and development between people born in poverty in rural areas vs middle-class people born in urban areas. In particular, SSL wanted to emphasize the differences in health and access to health services.

The target audience was people in government positions in Huancavelica. And the main objective was to make the residents aware of these differences, how they affect people and the benefits of combating them using territorial health management.

The project had the following requirements:

  • The video had to be simple to understand and tell a story.
  • Visually, the video had to mix illustration and real-life interviews.
  • All the illustrations had to represent Huancavelica and its people.

The Process

My role in this project was to create all the graphic assets for the video. I built the visual identity based on educational illustrations, using a grayscale palette with blue and orange accents to emphasize the contrast between the two protagonists. Each accent colour represented a value (Orange = sick, blue = healthy)

The biggest challenge was to build a visual identity that felt close to the target audience population. I used references and photographs provided by the client to arrive at a faithful representation of the culture, its buildings and people. The video's characters include a range of different members of the community. I also had to put special care in the portrayal of the community's customs.
